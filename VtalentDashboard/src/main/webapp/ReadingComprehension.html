Paragraph 3

In recent decades, the rapid advancement of science and technology has dramatically altered the way human societies function. From instant communication through smartphones and the internet, to breakthroughs in medical research and renewable energy, technology has become an inseparable part of our everyday lives. Yet, these developments also raise a series of ethical and social questions. While automation and artificial intelligence can increase efficiency and reduce human error, they may also lead to large-scale unemployment and a widening gap between the rich and the poor. The issue of privacy has also become critical, as personal data is constantly being collected, stored, and analyzed by corporations and governments. In education, technology has opened doors to learning resources beyond imagination, but excessive screen time and overreliance on digital platforms may hinder the development of interpersonal skills and critical thinking. Similarly, though medical technology has prolonged human life expectancy, it has also given rise to debates about affordability, access, and ethical limits of genetic engineering. The true challenge before humanity is to strike a balance: to ensure that technological progress not only drives economic growth and convenience but also promotes equity, sustainability, and the well-being of all people. Ultimately, technology should serve as a tool to empower humanity rather than dominate it.
